About Reiki

Reiki is a gentle, non-invasive energy healing practice that originated in Japan in the early 20th century. It was developed by Dr Mikao Usui, whose work laid the foundation for what is now widely known as Usui Reiki. Since then, Reiki has evolved into both Eastern and Western lineages, each maintaining the same core principle of supporting the body’s natural ability to restore balance.

I am trained in both the Eastern and Western Reiki traditions, allowing me to work in a way that is both intuitive and structured. This combined approach supports a grounded and professional standard of practice, suitable for a range of environments, including clinical settings such as those found within the NHS.

My training was completed at the Reiki Science Academy in London, where the focus is on delivering Reiki with clarity, integrity, and a strong understanding of both its traditional roots and modern applications.

Reiki is a non-manipulative complementary therapy, meaning it works alongside other treatments rather than replacing them. It does not involve physical manipulation of the body and instead focuses on supporting the flow of energy to encourage relaxation and overall wellbeing.

Sessions can be carried out either hands-on or hands-off, depending on your preference and comfort. Both approaches are equally valid and are always guided by what feels most appropriate for you.

Reiki has been found to be particularly supportive in helping individuals manage stress-related conditions such as anxiety, depression, and burn-out. It is also used as a complementary approach in areas such as addiction recovery and palliative care, where creating a sense of calm, comfort, and emotional balance is especially important.

Each session is approached with care, respect, and sensitivity, creating a safe and peaceful space for you to relax, restore, and reconnect.
