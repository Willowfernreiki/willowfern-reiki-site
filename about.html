About Reiki

Reiki is a gentle, non-invasive energy healing practice that originated in Japan in the early 20th century. It was developed by Dr Mikao Usui, whose teachings form the foundation of Usui Reiki. Since then, Reiki has developed into both Eastern and Western lineages, while maintaining its core principle of supporting the body’s natural ability to restore balance.

I am trained in both the Eastern and Western Reiki traditions, allowing me to work in a way that combines intuitive sensitivity with structured, grounded practice. This dual-lineage training supports a professional standard of work that is suitable for a wide range of environments, including complementary clinical settings aligned with NHS wellbeing approaches.

My training was completed at the Reiki Science Academy in London, where the focus is on delivering Reiki with clarity, integrity, and an evidence-informed understanding of both traditional and modern applications.

Reiki is a non-manipulative complementary therapy. This means it is used alongside conventional care and does not replace medical treatment. It does not involve physical manipulation of the body and instead supports relaxation and the natural flow of energy within the system.

Sessions can be offered either hands-on or hands-off, depending entirely on your comfort and preference. Both approaches are equally effective and are guided by consent and individual need.

Reiki is widely used as a supportive practice in areas such as stress, anxiety, depression, burnout, addiction recovery, and palliative care. While not a medical treatment, it is often chosen as a complementary therapy to support emotional wellbeing, relaxation, and comfort during challenging periods.
